Our Team

The people behind the Connecticut Citizens' Assembly — from the expert governance committee that ensures methodological rigor, to the project team that brings the assembly to life.

Interim Expert Governance Committee — Leadership
Hélène Landemore
Chair, Interim Expert Governance Committee
Yale University
Hélène Landemore is a leading scholar of deliberative and participatory democracy and will serve as Chair of the Interim Expert Governance Committee.
Sean Scanlon
State Comptroller
State of Connecticut
Connecticut State Comptroller Sean Scanlon has committed to endorse the assembly process and bring its recommendations to a dedicated legislative hearing, leveraging his constitutional authority on financial issues.
Joe DeLong
Executive Director
Connecticut Conference of Municipalities (CCM)
Joe DeLong serves as Executive Director of CCM and brings extensive experience in Connecticut municipal policy, local government, and statewide civic engagement.
Joshua Kalla
Associate Professor of Political Science & Statistics
Yale University
Joshua Kalla is an expert in political behavior and field experimentation, bringing rigorous empirical methods to the study of civic engagement and public opinion.
Michael E. Morrell
Associate Professor of Political Science
University of Connecticut
Michael Morrell researches deliberative democracy, political psychology, and civic participation, with deep expertise in how citizens engage with complex policy questions.
Project Management
Gryffin Wilkens-Plumley
Assistant Project Manager · Design Lead
Gryffin has been a project manager and design lead for the Connecticut Citizens' Assembly from its inception during summer 2025. He has experience working for Missions Publiques as a strategic consultant on their initiative for a Global Citizens' Dialogue on AI, and has presented on deliberative democracy and technology at conferences in Geneva, Washington, at Yale, and more. Gryffin also participated in design and facilitation for a Yale Citizens' Assembly in 2024–2025.
Committee Members
Alexander Guerrero
Rutgers University
Alexander Guerrero is a Professor of Philosophy at Rutgers University – New Brunswick. He is the author of Lottocracy: Democracy Without Elections (Oxford University Press, 2024), defending a political system that operates entirely through randomly selected citizens. He has a JD and PhD from NYU and an AB from Harvard.
Ariel Procaccia
Harvard University
Ariel Procaccia is the Alfred and Rebecca Lin Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University. He has helped create systems widely used to solve fair division problems, resettle refugees, and select citizens’ assemblies. He is an ACM Fellow (2025) and AAAI Fellow (2024), and has received the Guggenheim Fellowship (2018) and Sloan Research Fellowship (2015).
Audrey Tang
Cyber Ambassador, Taiwan
Audrey Tang is a civic hacker and technologist who rewires systems for the public good. As Taiwan’s first digital minister, she helped shape the 2014 Sunflower Movement and created vTaiwan, a participatory platform mapping agreement across diverse opinions. She now pioneers civic-minded approaches to AI, from Oxford’s Institute for Ethics in AI to safety collaborations with global platforms.
Cynthia Farrar
Purple States TV
Cynthia Farrar is an independent scholar and civic entrepreneur. She organized the Citizens Forum (2002–2007), a series of public deliberations among randomly invited citizens across Greater New Haven. In 2007 she co-founded Purple States TV. She is co-author of the 2nd edition of The Origins of Democratic Thinking (Cambridge University Press).
Daniel Selz
$1 Billion for Trust
Dan Selz is co-founder of $1 Billion for Trust, a nonprofit focused on rebuilding trust in information, institutions, and each other. Previously he was Director of Innovation at the Leadership Now Project and spent nearly a decade at the XPRIZE Foundation, including designing the $100M Carbon Removal XPRIZE.
Hélène Landemore
Yale University
Hélène Landemore is a professor of political science at Yale University specializing in democratic theory and political epistemology. She is a fellow at Oxford’s Ethics in AI Institute, an advisor to OpenAI’s Democratic Inputs to AI program, and served on the Governance Committee of the most recent French Citizens’ Convention.
Jane Suiter
Dublin City University
Jane Suiter is Professor of Political Communication at Dublin City University and Director of DCU’s Institute for Future Media, Democracy and Society. She has been involved in the Irish Citizens’ Assembly (2012–2022), was a founding member of We the Citizens (2011), and was named the Irish Research Council’s Researcher of the Year in 2020.
Joshua Kalla
Yale University
Josh Kalla is Associate Professor of Political Science at Yale University with a secondary appointment in Statistics and Data Science. His research studies political persuasion, prejudice reduction, and decision-making among voters and political elites, primarily through the use of randomized field experiments.
JT Flowers
Albina Vision Trust
JT Flowers earned his BA from Yale and made history as the first Black and first Native Hawaiian Rhodes Scholar in Oregon’s history, holding master’s degrees in U.S. History and Music Performance from Oxford. He serves as Senior Advisor at the Albina Vision Trust and on TriMet’s Board of Directors.
Lunda Asmani
Norwalk Public Schools; GFOA
Lunda Asmani, CPFO, is Chief Financial Officer for Norwalk Public Schools, leading financial strategy for a $300 million budget. He serves as President of both the national Government Finance Officers Association and the Connecticut GFOA, and is a champion for advancing best practices in public finance.
Michiel Bakker
MIT; Google DeepMind
Michiel Bakker is an Assistant Professor at MIT and Research Scientist at Google DeepMind. His most prominent work is the "Habermas Machine," published in Science, showing that AI can help humans find common ground in democratic deliberation. He holds degrees in physics from TU Delft and a Computer Science PhD from MIT.
Michael E. Morrell
University of Connecticut
Michael E. Morrell is Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Connecticut, studying democracy and deliberation for three decades. His focus is the interplay between deliberation and emotions — especially the role of empathy. He contributed to the Citizens Initiative Review Research Project and received his PhD from Arizona State University in 1998.
Oliver Hart
Harvard University
Oliver Hart is the Lewis P. and Linda L. Geyser University Professor at Harvard, where he has taught since 1993. He is the 2016 co-recipient of the Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences. His research centers on the governance and boundaries of corporations, and his recent work focuses on the social responsibility of business.
Peter MacLeod
MASS LBP
Peter MacLeod is co-founder and principal of Mass LBP, one of Canada’s leading experts in public engagement and deliberative democracy. Under his leadership, Mass LBP pioneered the use of Civic Lotteries and Citizen Reference Panels. He is an Action Canada fellow and 2008 recipient of the Public Policy Forum’s Emerging Leaders Award.
Sophie Guillain
Res publica
Trained as an urban planner, Sophie Guillain is Managing Director and Partner of Res publica, a consulting firm specializing in collective intelligence and public participation. She leads stakeholder management for national and regional public policies across France and abroad, and teaches public participation engineering at the Sorbonne – Paris I.
Zephyr Teachout
Fordham Law School
Zephyr Teachout is Professor of Law at Fordham Law School, focusing on corporate power and political power. Her books include Break ‘em Up (2020) and Corruption in America (2014). In 2021 she served as Special Advisor and Senior Counsel for Economic Justice at the New York Attorney General’s Office.

Our Team

The people behind the Connecticut Citizens' Assembly — from the expert governance committee that ensures methodological rigor, to the project team that brings the assembly to life.

Interim Expert Governance Committee — Leadership
Hélène Landemore
Chair, Interim Expert Governance Committee
Yale University
Hélène Landemore is a leading scholar of deliberative and participatory democracy and will serve as Chair of the Interim Expert Governance Committee.
Sean Scanlon
State Comptroller
State of Connecticut
Connecticut State Comptroller Sean Scanlon has committed to endorse the assembly process and bring its recommendations to a dedicated legislative hearing, leveraging his constitutional authority on financial issues.
Joe DeLong
Executive Director
Connecticut Conference of Municipalities (CCM)
Joe DeLong serves as Executive Director of CCM and brings extensive experience in Connecticut municipal policy, local government, and statewide civic engagement.
Joshua Kalla
Associate Professor of Political Science & Statistics
Yale University
Joshua Kalla is an expert in political behavior and field experimentation, bringing rigorous empirical methods to the study of civic engagement and public opinion.
Michael E. Morrell
Associate Professor of Political Science
University of Connecticut
Michael Morrell researches deliberative democracy, political psychology, and civic participation, with deep expertise in how citizens engage with complex policy questions.
Project Management
Gryffin Wilkens-Plumley
Assistant Project Manager · Design Lead
Gryffin has been a project manager and design lead for the Connecticut Citizens' Assembly from its inception during summer 2025. He has experience working for Missions Publiques as a strategic consultant on their initiative for a Global Citizens' Dialogue on AI, and has presented on deliberative democracy and technology at conferences in Geneva, Washington, at Yale, and more. Gryffin also participated in design and facilitation for a Yale Citizens' Assembly in 2024–2025.
Committee Members

Alexander Guerrero
Rutgers University
+
Alexander Guerrero is a Professor of Philosophy at Rutgers University – New Brunswick, with a secondary appointment at Rutgers Law School. His research and teaching focuses on political philosophy, ethics, legal philosophy, and epistemology. He is the author of Lottocracy: Democracy Without Elections (Oxford University Press, 2024), which presents and defends a political system that operates entirely through the use of randomly selected citizens. He has a JD and PhD from New York University and an AB from Harvard.
Ariel Procaccia
Harvard University
+
Ariel Procaccia is the Alfred and Rebecca Lin Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University. He works on problems related to AI, algorithms, economics, and society. He has helped create systems widely used to solve fair division problems, resettle refugees, distribute food, and select citizens' assemblies. He is an ACM Fellow (2025), AAAI Fellow (2024), and recipient of the Guggenheim Fellowship (2018) and Sloan Research Fellowship (2015), among other honors.
Audrey Tang
Cyber Ambassador, Taiwan
+
Audrey Tang is a civic hacker and technologist who rewires systems for the public good. As Taiwan's cyber ambassador and first digital minister, she showed how technology could deepen trust, giving millions a direct role in shaping policy. She co-shaped the 2014 Sunflower Movement and created vTaiwan, a participatory platform mapping agreement across diverse opinions. She now pioneers civic-minded approaches to AI, from Oxford's Institute for Ethics in AI to safety collaborations with global platforms.
Claire Priest
Yale Law School
+
Claire Priest is Dean and Sol & Lillian Goldman Professor of Law at Yale Law School. Her scholarship focuses on American legal history, property law, and the intersection of law and democracy. She brings expertise in institutional governance and legal scholarship to the Interim Expert Governance Committee.
Cynthia Farrar
Purple States TV
+
Cynthia Farrar is an independent scholar and civic entrepreneur who has lived in New Haven for 40 years. Working with James Fishkin, she organized the Citizens Forum, a series of public deliberations among randomly invited citizens across Greater New Haven (2002–2007). In 2007 she co-founded Purple States TV to bring the voices of people affected by policy choices into public discussion. She is co-author of the 2nd edition of The Origins of Democratic Thinking (Cambridge University Press).
Daniel Selz
$1 Billion for Trust
+
Dan Selz is co-founder of $1 Billion for Trust, a nonprofit focused on growing a 9-million-person community to collectively fund solutions that rebuild trust in information, institutions, and each other. Previously he was Director of Innovation at the Leadership Now Project and spent nearly a decade at the XPRIZE Foundation, including designing the $100M Carbon Removal XPRIZE.
Jane Suiter
Dublin City University
+
Jane Suiter is Professor of Political Communication at Dublin City University and Director of DCU's Institute for Future Media, Democracy and Society. Her research focuses on scaling up deliberation and tackling disinformation. She has been involved in the Irish Citizens' Assembly (2012–2022), was a founding member of We the Citizens (2011), and has advised on citizens' assemblies in Scotland and elsewhere. She was named the Irish Research Council's Researcher of the Year in 2020.
JT Flowers
Albina Vision Trust
+
JT Flowers is a lifelong Portlander born in inner-NE Portland's historic Albina district. He earned his BA in Political Science with distinction from Yale University and made history as the first Black and first Native Hawaiian Rhodes Scholar in Oregon's history, earning master's degrees in U.S. History and Music Performance from Oxford. He serves as Senior Advisor at the Albina Vision Trust and on TriMet's Board of Directors representing District 5.
Lunda Asmani
Norwalk Public Schools; GFOA
+
Lunda Asmani, CPFO, is Chief Financial Officer for Norwalk Public Schools in Connecticut, leading financial strategy and operations for a $300 million budget. With nearly two decades of municipal leadership experience across Connecticut and Kansas, he also serves as President of both the national Government Finance Officers Association and the Connecticut Government Finance Officers Association. He is a champion for advancing best practices in public finance.
Michiel Bakker
MIT; Google DeepMind
+
Michiel Bakker is an Assistant Professor at MIT and Research Scientist at Google DeepMind. His research focuses on large language models, AI safety, and AI's role in human-human interaction. His most prominent work is the "Habermas Machine," published in Science, showing that AI can help humans find common ground in democratic deliberation. He holds a bachelor's and master's in physics from TU Delft and a Computer Science PhD from MIT.
Michael E. Morrell
University of Connecticut
+
Michael E. Morrell is Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Connecticut, where he has studied democracy and deliberation for three decades. His major area of interest is the interplay between deliberation and emotions, especially the role of empathy in democracy. He has observed deliberative mini-publics in New Haven and contributed to the Citizens Initiative Review Research Project, designing participant surveys and observing citizens discussing ballot referenda. He received his PhD from Arizona State University in 1998.
Oliver Hart
Harvard University
+
Oliver Hart is the Lewis P. and Linda L. Geyser University Professor at Harvard University, where he has taught since 1993. He is the 2016 co-recipient of the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel. His research centers on the roles that ownership structure and contractual arrangements play in the governance and boundaries of corporations, and his recent work focuses on the social responsibility of business.
Peter MacLeod
MASS LBP
+
Peter MacLeod is co-founder and principal of Mass LBP, one of Canada's leading experts in public engagement and deliberative democracy. Under his leadership, Mass LBP has pioneered the use of Civic Lotteries and Citizen Reference Panels for governments across Canada. A former researcher at Britain's Demos think tank, he writes and speaks about the citizen's experience of the state and the future of responsible government. He is an Action Canada fellow and 2008 recipient of the Public Policy Forum's Emerging Leaders Award.
Sophie Guillain
Res publica
+
Trained as an urban planner, Sophie Guillain is Managing Director and Partner of Res publica, a consulting firm specializing in collective intelligence and public participation. She leads stakeholder management missions for national and regional public policies across sustainable development, transportation, food, and sports. She works with public officials at all levels of local government in France and abroad, and teaches public participation engineering at the Sorbonne – Paris I.
Zephyr Teachout
Fordham Law School
+
Zephyr Teachout is Professor of Law at Fordham Law School, focusing on the intersection of corporate power and political power. She teaches corporations, election law, antitrust, and prosecuting white collar crime. Her books include Break 'em Up (2020) and Corruption in America (2014). In 2021 she served as Special Advisor and Senior Counsel for Economic Justice at the New York Attorney General's Office.

The Question

"Should Connecticut change how we pay for and deliver local services like education, road maintenance, or public safety?"

You do not need any special background knowledge. Participants will learn together — hearing from experts, community members, and one another — before reaching conclusions.

Compensation

$1,200

for full participation in the Connecticut Citizens' Assembly.

When & How

  • Assembly sessions run July – September 2026.
  • 100 participants chosen by random lottery from all who respond.
  • Recommendations presented directly to the Connecticut Legislature.

Who Is Behind This

A nonpartisan effort led by Connecticut Comptroller Sean Scanlon and the Connecticut Conference of Municipalities, in partnership with Yale University and the University of Connecticut.
